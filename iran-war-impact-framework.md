**Key developments today:**
- 🕊️ **45-day ceasefire proposal now active — first real diplomatic track of the war** — Mediators (Oman, Qatar, Pakistan intermediaries) presented both the US and Iran with a two-phase peace framework: Phase 1 = a 45-day ceasefire (extendable) during which a permanent end to the war is negotiated; Phase 2 = final deal covering Hormuz reopening and disposition of Iran's highly enriched uranium stockpile. Iran says it has "formulated its response" to the ceasefire proposals but will not engage in direct talks while US/Israeli attacks continue. This is the most significant diplomatic development of the war — more structured than the China-Pakistan proposal (April 1) — and is the primary reason Brent did NOT gap up to $115–125 at Monday's open. (Sources: Axios, Reuters, Bloomberg, NBC News)

President Trump announced the rescue early Sunday. The airman was hiding in a mountain crevice and could still walk despite his injuries. Both F-15E crew members are now safe. This removes the war's biggest single domestic US political escalation trigger — the prospect of an American POW in Iranian hands. (Sources: Axios, Time, WaPo, Fox News)

Iran rejected the ultimatum, insisting the strait reopens only when it is "fully compensated" for war damages. The shift from April 6 to April 8 gave ceasefire mediators a critical 48-hour window. Iran's foreign ministry called Trump's threats "adventurism" and warned of "much more devastating" retaliation if civilian infrastructure is struck. (Sources: CNN, NPR, Al Jazeera liveblog)

Five escalation scenarios with current probability weightings and key trigger events.

*US/Israeli strikes with Iranian retaliation, no major shipping disruption, proxies restrained*

*Current state: traffic severely reduced, US campaign to reopen ongoing, war-risk premiums extreme*

- **Triggers down:** Mining of strait, attack on US naval vessel, full Hezbollah ground war in Israel

### S3 — Regional War / Full Proxy Activation (32%)
*Hezbollah opens full northern front in Israel, Iraqi militia attacks escalate to strategic targets, Houthis resurge*
- **Triggers up:** Israeli ground invasion of Lebanon, US base casualties exceed threshold, Hezbollah missile hits Israeli civilian infrastructure
- **Triggers down:** Hezbollah ceasefire deal, Iran proxy command disrupted by strikes, Iraqi government crackdown on militia (Lebanon gov ban on Hezbollah is a new downward trigger — Apr 1)

### S4 — Nuclear Dimension (10%)
*Israeli or US strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities, or Iranian nuclear coercion*
- **Triggers up:** Evidence Iran attempting to weaponize fissile material, Israeli domestic political pressure for "final solution" to nuclear threat
- **Triggers down:** Iranian nuclear leadership killed/disrupted (partially occurred), IAEA verification of program halt

### S5 — Great Power Entanglement (14%)
*Russia or China provide material military support to Iran, creating direct US confrontation risk*
- **Triggers up:** US strike on Iranian asset supported by Russian intelligence, China deploys naval assets near strait, UNSC deadlock leads to unilateral actions
- **Triggers down:** Back-channel US-China agreement, Russia chooses economic over military alignment (China now taking diplomatic mediator role — Apr 1; Russia confirmed providing intelligence but not material military assets)

## Historical Analogues

| Analogue | Year | Oil Spike | Peak Price | Duration | GDP Impact | Recession? | Lessons |
|----------|------|-----------|-----------|----------|------------|------------|---------|
| Arab Oil Embargo | 1973–74 | +300% | $12/bbl (from $3) | 12 months | −3.2% US GDP | Yes (1974–75) | Supply shock alone creates severe stagflation; strategic reserves became policy tool |
| Iran Revolution | 1979–80 | +130% | $35/bbl | 18 months | −2% global | Yes (double-dip) | Political disruption to producing nation even without full blockade causes severe shock |
| Iraq invades Kuwait | 1990 | +100% | $40/bbl | 6 months | −0.5% global | Mild | Short sharp shock; quick resolution limits long-term damage; SPR release effective |
| Abqaiq Attack | 2019 | +15% (brief) | $65/bbl | Days | Negligible | No | Even largest infrastructure attack had limited duration impact without sustained conflict |
| Russia-Ukraine War | 2022 | +80% | $130/bbl | 18 months | −3% EU GDP | Near-recession | Energy weapon as strategic tool; accelerated EU transition; showed EM vulnerability |

**Key calibration from analogues:**
- Average duration of oil shocks that caused recessions: 12–18 months of elevated prices
- Strategic reserve releases historically reduce price by $5–15/bbl temporarily but do not solve structural supply issues
- Rapid resolution (1990 Gulf War model) can normalize prices within months — key variable is negotiated outcome timing
- Stagflation risk is highest when shock coincides with pre-existing inflation (current US CPI already elevated)
