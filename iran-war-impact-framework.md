**Key developments today:**
- 💣 **US strikes Kharg Island military targets — Iran's oil export hub directly engaged for first time** — The US military struck military bunkers, air defense systems, and storage facilities on Kharg Island, which handles ~90% of Iran's oil exports. US and White House officials confirmed the mission included airstrikes along the island's northern side and did NOT include ground troops. Critically, US officials stressed that oil infrastructure was NOT targeted — the strikes hit military command and air defense capabilities only. However, oil markets reacted immediately: WTI jumped ~3% to ~$116/bbl on the news, and Brent rose above $113/bbl. Iran's IRGC warned that "restraint is over" following the strike and threatened to target Gulf oil infrastructure. Iran also threatened to strike a UAE AI center after a separate US strike hit Tehran's Sharif University of Technology. (Sources: NBC News, Al-Monitor, Haaretz, CNBC)
- 🔥 **Israel strikes South Pars gas field — 85% of Iranian petrochemical output now offline** — Israel struck a major petrochemical facility at the South Pars natural gas field (the world's largest, shared with Qatar), killing two IRGC commanders. Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz confirmed "a powerful strike on the largest petrochemical facility in Iran," responsible for half the country's petrochemical production. Combined with the April 4 Mahshahr strike (which took the second-largest petrochemical zone offline), approximately 85% of Iran's total petrochemical output is now offline. South Pars is also the source of Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG — the strike has structural implications for Qatar's LNG restoration timeline well beyond the Hormuz closure. Iran responded with missile strikes; Iranian missiles killed 4 people in a Haifa residential building. (Sources: Washington Times, Military.com, CBS News, Daily Pioneer, Tribune India)
- 📋 **Iran issues 10-point permanent-peace counter-proposal — rejects 45-day ceasefire** — Iran formally rejected the 45-day ceasefire proposal and submitted a 10-point counter-proposal to mediator Pakistan, which includes: (1) a protocol for safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz, (2) reconstruction of war-affected areas, (3) lifting of international sanctions, (4) ending all regional conflicts (Lebanon, Yemen), and (5) a path to permanently end the war. Iran's FM spokesperson Baghaei said no "sane" person would accept a temporary ceasefire that gives opponents time to regroup. Trump called Iran's 10-point response "a significant step" but "not good enough." This is the first time Iran has explicitly proposed a Hormuz safe-passage protocol as a negotiating offer — a notable shift from the absolute "Hormuz sovereignty" position — but the gap to US demands (full reopening, missile program halt, nuclear shutdown) remains vast. Iran is also seeking Russia and China to be involved in any agreement to verify the US isn't using talks as a "deception tactic." (Sources: Xinhua, CNBC, RFERL, Kyiv Post, Times of Israel)

Trump also widened his conditions to include Iran reopening Hormuz, halting ballistic missile development, AND completely shutting down its nuclear program. ## Scenario Ladder

Five escalation scenarios with current probability weightings and key trigger events.

### S1 — Limited Air Campaign (14%)
*US/Israeli strikes with Iranian retaliation, no major shipping disruption, proxies restrained*
- **Triggers up:** Iran negotiated ceasefire, Hormuz fully reopened, proxy ceasefire
- **Triggers down:** Any vessel attack, Hormuz mining, Hezbollah ground operation

### S2 — Partial Hormuz Disruption — ACTIVE SCENARIO (35%)
*Current state: traffic severely reduced, US campaign to reopen ongoing, war-risk premiums extreme*
- **Triggers up:** Peace deal, full reopening, proxy disengagement
- **Triggers down:** Mining of strait, attack on US naval vessel, full Hezbollah ground war in Israel

### S3 — Regional War / Full Proxy Activation (40%)
*Hezbollah opens full northern front in Israel, Iraqi militia attacks escalate to strategic targets, Houthis resurge*
- **Triggers up:** Israeli ground invasion of Lebanon, US base casualties exceed threshold, Hezbollah missile hits Israeli civilian infrastructure
- **Triggers down:** Hezbollah ceasefire deal, Iran proxy command disrupted by strikes, Iraqi government crackdown on militia (Lebanon gov ban on Hezbollah is a new downward trigger — Apr 1)

### S4 — Nuclear Dimension (13%)
*Israeli or US strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities, or Iranian nuclear coercion*
- **Triggers up:** Evidence Iran attempting to weaponize fissile material, Israeli domestic political pressure for "final solution" to nuclear threat
- **Triggers down:** Iranian nuclear leadership killed/disrupted (partially occurred), IAEA verification of program halt

### S5 — Great Power Entanglement (14%)
*Russia or China provide material military support to Iran, creating direct US confrontation risk*
- **Triggers up:** US strike on Iranian asset supported by Russian intelligence, China deploys naval assets near strait, UNSC deadlock leads to unilateral actions
- **Triggers down:** Back-channel US-China agreement, Russia chooses economic over military alignment (China now taking diplomatic mediator role — Apr 1; Russia confirmed providing intelligence but not material military assets)

- Strategic reserve releases historically reduce price by $5–15/bbl temporarily but do not solve structural supply issues
- Rapid resolution (1990 Gulf War model) can normalize prices within months — key variable is negotiated outcome timing
- Stagflation risk is highest when shock coincides with pre-existing inflation (current US CPI already elevated)
